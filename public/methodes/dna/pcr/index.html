Procedure:

Calculating the annealing temperature and elongation duration for the GOI:

Annealing Temperature Calculation

The annealing temperature Ta can be calculated from the melting temperature Tm or looked up within SnapGene.

Tm = 4×(GC-Pairing) + 2×(AT-Pairing)
Ta = Tm– 4

Elongation time for Phusion HS II is 30–60 sec/1kb.

te = bp/1000 × 30 sec






Preparing the Thermocycler:

  1. Enter the Programm into the Termocycler:

CNTRL BLOCK
LID=105°
NOWAIT AUTO
1 T=98.0°C 0:00:30 (initial denaturation)
2 T=98.0°C 0:00:10 (cycle1: denaturation)
3 T=xx°C 0:00:30 (cycle1: primer annealing)
4 T=72.0°C 0:xx:xx (cycle1: elongation) 5 GOTO 2 REP 19 (sum: 20 cycles)
6 T=72.0°C 0:08:00 (final incubation)
7 HOLD 4°C
End

Ta: °C

te: seconds

Preparing the Samples and Running the Program:

ℹ️ Prepare a mastermix when doing multiple samples containing dsH2O, GC buffer, DMSO and dNTP. Use number of samples plus at least one for the mastermix volume multiplyer.
  1. Prepare a Mastermix:
Volume V [µL] Reagent c1/m1 c2/m2
30.5 µL  dsH2O
10 µL GC buffer 5x 1x
1.5 µL DMSO 100 % 
1 µL dNTP mix 10 mM 200 µM
= 43 µL

Number of samples:

  1. Prepare samples with mastermix by pipetting the following volumes into a 0.5 mL PCR tube:
Volume V [µL] Reagent c1/m1 c2/m2
43 µL   Mastermix  
2.5 µL Forward Primer 10 µM 0.5µM
2.5 µL Reverse Primer 10 µM 0.5µM
1 µL DNA Template 10 ng/µL  200 pg/µL
1 µL Phusion Hot Start II DNA Polymerase 2 U/µL 2 U
= 50 µL

Alternatively prepare samples individually by pipetting the following volumes into a 0.5 mL PCR tube:

Volume V [µL] Reagent c1/m1 c2/m2
30.5 µL  dsH2O
10 µL GC buffer 5x 1x
1.5 µL DMSO 100 % 
1 µL dNTP mix 10 mM 200 µM
2.5 µL Forward Primer 10 µM 0.5µM
2.5 µL Reverse Primer 10 µM 0.5µM
1 µL DNA Template 10 ng/µL  200 pg/µL
1 µL Phusion Hot Start II DNA Polymerase 2 U/µL 2 U
= 50 µL
  1. Place the PCR tubes into the Thermocycler with maximum disance between samples.
  2. Start the PCR program.
